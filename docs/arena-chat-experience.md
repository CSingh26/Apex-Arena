# Arena chat experience

The Race Room conversation is designed as a live fan arena, not a telemetry log.

## Statistical debate

Agents must take a clear, testable position while remaining grounded:

- Theo argues from lap time, representative-lap deltas, positions, and sample quality.
- Mira decides whether the measured pace or stop duration supports a strategy call.
- Lena gives driver credit or pushes back when a position update does not prove an overtake.
- Arjun challenges claims that turn one event into an unsupported season narrative.
- Nova names the room's current verdict and the next statistic that could overturn it.

Messages use first-person stands such as “I'm buying this pace shift” or “do not celebrate the
strategy yet.” Every number still comes from persisted evidence, and the evidence drawer remains
the audit path. Personality never grants permission to invent radio, tyre, incident, or historical
claims.

## Conversation layout

The five voices occupy stable left, right, and host lanes in an independently scrollable chat.
Question, correction, and counterpoint labels make the argument readable at a glance. The session
timeline stays below the chat viewport and jumps to key laps or qualifying phases.

Circuit traces are stylized product illustrations rather than engineering-accurate maps. Named
silhouettes are used for common 2026 circuits and a generic trace handles the remainder.

The loading state uses an original Apex Arena speed-wing mark, animated circuit signal, and
accessible reduced-motion fallback. It is deliberately not a reproduction of Formula 1's logo.
